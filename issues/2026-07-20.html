[ P2 · POLICY & JUSTICE ]

Supreme Court Protects Lisa Cook's Fed Role From Trump

The U.S. Supreme Court ruled that Lisa Cook, the first Black woman to serve on the Federal Reserve Board, may retain her position as she challenges President Trump's unlawful attempt to fire her without just cause. The Court ruled that the President can only remove Fed members if they receive basic due process, including the right to respond to allegations of misconduct.

Sources [NAACP Legal Defense Fund] [NPR]
[ P5 · HBCUS & EDUCATION ]

HBCU Leaders Convene in Atlanta for UNITE 2026

UNCF announced UNITE 2026, the nation's most influential annual gathering of HBCU leaders, advocates, funders and thought leaders. The event convenes July 19–23 at the Signia by Hilton Atlanta, bringing together more than 1,000 attendees including a record number of current and former HBCU presidents and representatives from 300+ organizations.

Sources [Atlanta Tribune] [mycarrollcountynews.com]
[ P11 · BLACK EXCELLENCE ]

Patti LaBelle Honored as Culture Icon at Essence Fest

Legendary singer Patti LaBelle received the Culture Icon Award at the sixth Black Excellence Brunch during the 2026 ESSENCE Festival in New Orleans. At 82 years young, Ms. Patti radiated with gratitude while receiving flowers for her indelible impact on pop culture and timeless influence on generations of artists and audiences.

Sources [Essence] [Black America Web]
