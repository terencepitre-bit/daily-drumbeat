NYC Council passes $2.1M equity funding, expands housing protections

RACIAL EQUITY WINS — New York City Council Speaker Julie Menin announced major racial equity investments this week, allocating $2.1 million for the Commission on Racial Equity and expanding the City Fighting Homelessness and Eviction Prevention Supplement (CityFHEPS) program. The $125.8 billion budget also rejected a proposed 9.5% property tax increase on homeowners. The moves target persistent disparities: city data shows the median household net worth of white New Yorkers is nearly 15 times greater than that of Black New Yorkers.

The takeaway
NYC Council delivers concrete funding for racial equity as wealth gap remains stark.
