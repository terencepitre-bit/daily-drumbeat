NYC specialized high schools admit only 3.5% Black students despite 20% enrollment

EDUCATION WATCH — New York City's eight elite specialized high schools continued their pattern of severely underrepresenting Black and Latino students in admission decisions, data released this month revealed. Black students, who comprise nearly 20% of NYC's public school population, received just 3.5% of admissions offers—only three spots at the competitive Stuyvesant High School out of 777 freshman positions. The disparity persists despite ongoing criticism of the single-test admissions model, with Mayor Zohran Mamdani committing to review the entrance exam for racial and gender bias.

The takeaway
Nearly 70% of NYC public school students are Black or Latino, yet they capture just 10% of the crown jewel specialized school seats.
