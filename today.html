Freedom Summer 2026 mobilizes Black voters nationwide

VOTING RIGHTS — A nationwide coalition of civil rights, faith, labor, and community organizations launched Freedom Summer 2026 this month to defend voting rights and economic justice following the Supreme Court's gutting of Section 2 of the Voting Rights Act. The campaign, running from Juneteenth through Election Day, combines mass meetings, voter registration drives, church revivals, and direct action campaigns across the country. Organizers describe it as continuing the legacy of the 1964 Freedom Summer while responding to escalating attacks on Black political power, particularly in the South.

The takeaway
This coordinated summer of action represents the most organized Black voting rights pushback since the Supreme Court stripped federal oversight from nine Southern states.
