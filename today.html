Supreme Court Ruling Threatens Local Reparations Programs

POLICY & JUSTICE — The Department of Justice is intervening in a lawsuit challenging Evanston's local reparations program, posing a significant threat to grassroots efforts addressing racial wealth gaps across the country. The case represents a critical moment for municipalities experimenting with reparative justice. Legal experts warn that federal opposition could chill similar initiatives in other cities and states, particularly as Congressional efforts to advance HR 40—a federal commission to study reparations—remain stalled in the Republican-controlled Congress.

The takeaway
Federal resistance to local reparations efforts signals a troubling shift away from addressing systemic racial inequity through policy.
