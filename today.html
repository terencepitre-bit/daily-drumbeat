Supreme Court Decimates Voting Rights Protections for Black Voters

VOTING RIGHTS WATCH — The Supreme Court weakened Section 2 of the Voting Rights Act to the point of inoperability in a 6-3 ruling, striking down Louisiana's congressional map that was redrawn to create an additional district where Black voters could have the opportunity to elect their candidates of choice, resulting in the election of two Black Louisianians to Congress for the first time in history. States across the South have already begun special legislative sessions to redraw maps: Florida passed new maps within hours of the decision, and Tennessee drew and passed new maps just days later.

The takeaway
The ruling eliminates the primary federal tool Black voters have used to challenge racially discriminatory voting maps, opening the door for widespread disenfranchisement ahead of the 2026 midterms.
